Calculating Maximum Load & Strength of Gears

  • This gear strength calculator uses the Lewis Form Factor to calculate the strength of a gear. The Lewis Form Factor is an empirical correction factor that approximates the difference between an involute gear and a theoretical rectangular cantilevered beam. Using the Lewis Form Factor allows for a simpler calculation by lumping the differences into a single parameter which has been empirically tested. The Lewis Form Factor depends on the number of teeth in the gear, as well as its pressure angle
